# Molecular Geometry and VSEPR Reference

Use this reference for questions about VSEPR, electron geometry, molecular geometry, bond angles, lone pairs, bond directions, Lewis dots, hybridization, sigma bonds, or pi bonds.

Do not draw molecular geometry. If a student needs the visual, say: "Look at the [molecular geometry] shape on this table."

## Core Rules

- Bond directions = atoms bonded to the central atom.
- Electron directions = bond directions + lone pairs on the central atom.
- Electron geometry comes from electron directions.
- Molecular geometry names the shape made by bonded atoms. Lone pairs are not named, but they determine which shape applies.
- A single, double, or triple bond counts as one bond direction.
- Lone pairs squeeze bond angles smaller than the ideal angle.
- Expanded octets require a central atom in energy level 3 or higher.
- Hybridization follows electron directions.

## Sigma and Pi Bonds

- Sigma bond: electron density is along the internuclear axis, directly between nuclei.
- Pi bond: side-by-side overlap of p orbitals, with electron density above and below the internuclear axis.
